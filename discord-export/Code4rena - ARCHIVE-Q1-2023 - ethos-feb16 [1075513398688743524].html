Hello dear friends, I was wondering if someone could help me understand the difference between MCR and CCR in CDP-backed stablecoins. As far as I know, should the MCR be higher than CCR or vice versa?
uint256inc 02/17/2023 14:28
MCR (minimum collateralization ratio) is minimum CR at which you're allowed to open a trove while the system is in normal mode. Normal mode means that the total CR (across the whole market) is over the CCR (critical collateralization ratio). So for example, for the WBTC market, MCR is 120% and CCR is 150%. What this means is that as long as the total CR of the WBTC market is at least 150%, individual troves can fall as low as 120% and not be liquidated. But if the total CR of the market falls below 150%, recovery mode kicks in, and then there's special operations that are allowed (liquidating troves up to debt * MCR) on troves that sit between MCR and CCR. Basically, recovery mode turns on "strict mode" so that the system can push itself out of recovery mode as soon as possible.

One the core principles of the vault/strategy architecture is to have suspicion between the vault and the strategy. What I mean by this is that the vault doesn't just take the strategy's word for "this is how much money I have." The sum of these two should at least equal want balance within the strategy, but the vault would rather just trust the arguments it was invoked with.
